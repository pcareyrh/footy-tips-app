# NRL Tipping Decision Factors - Specification

## Overview
This document specifies comprehensive factors to consider when picking NRL teams for weekly tipping competitions. It covers quantitative (data-driven) and qualitative (judgment-based) factors at both team and player levels.

## Purpose
Create a foundational specification that can guide:
- Manual decision-making by tippers
- Development of analytical tools/apps for picking teams
- Systematic evaluation of team performance and matchups

---

## A. QUANTITATIVE FACTORS

### A1. Team Form & Momentum
- **Recent Win-Loss Record**: Last 4-6 weeks performance
- **Points For/Against**: Offensive vs defensive strength (recent)
- **Home/Away Form**: Venue-specific performance differences
- **Streaks**: Current winning/losing streak length and context
- **Trend Direction**: Improving vs declining trajectory

### A2. Head-to-Head History
- **Direct Matchup Record**: Historical win-loss vs opponent
- **Recent Matchups**: Last 2-3 encounters (form varies season to season)
- **Scoring Margins**: Typical point differentials in past meetings
- **Home Advantage Effect**: H2H performance at specific venues

### A3. Ladder Position & Context
- **Current Ladder Position**: Relative standings (top 8 contenders vs lower teams)
- **Points Differential**: Cumulative +/- points (quality of wins/losses)
- **Strength of Schedule**: Upcoming fixture difficulty vs past opponents faced
- **Mathematical Scenarios**: Pressure/motivation from finals positioning

### A4. Injury & Availability Status
- **Key Player Injuries**: Impact of star players, experienced leaders out
- **Injury Severity**: Game-changing positions (halfback, hooker, fullback vs bench depth)
- **Return Timeline**: When injured players expected back
- **Depth Quality**: Bench strength and replacement capability
- **Cumulative Absences**: Multiple injuries compounding effect

### A5. Playing Statistics
- **Completion Rate**: Ball security and efficiency
- **Tackling Accuracy**: Defensive effectiveness percentage
- **Offload Success**: Attacking structure and fluidity
- **Set Restarts**: Discipline and error rates
- **Possession %**: Game control metrics
- **Error Differential**: Handling vs opposition errors

### A6. Positional Matchups
- **Forward Pack Strength**: Size, aggression, experience in ruck
- **Backline Playmakers**: Halfback form and support play
- **Outside Backs**: Speed, decision-making, defensive reliability
- **Hooker Impact**: Dummy half involvement in attack/defense

## B. QUALITATIVE FACTORS

### B1. Coaching & Management
- **Coach Experience**: Playoff-tested, proven track record
- **Coaching Changes**: New coaches impact (learning curve vs fresh approach)
- **Game Plan Adjustments**: Tactical adaptations shown in recent games
- **Staff Stability**: Backroom changes affecting team cohesion
- **Motivation/Direction**: Interim coaches, club instability signals

### B2. Team Cohesion & Culture
- **Chemistry**: First-year players integrating vs settled combinations
- **Leadership Quality**: Captaincy, senior player influence
- **Resilience**: How teams respond to adversity/setbacks in-game
- **Communication**: On-field organization and call-making
- **Morale**: Confidence indicators, body language observations

### B3. Mental/Psychological Factors
- **Confidence Levels**: Swagger in winning teams vs tentative losing teams
- **Pressure Handling**: Performance in crucial moments vs cruising
- **Desperation**: Lower-ladder teams fighting for relevance vs settled top teams
- **Revenge Factor**: Previous losses creating motivation
- **Big Match Experience**: Playoff intensity vs regular season mentality

### B4. Referee & Match Dynamics
- **Referee Tendencies**: Known strictness on penalties, interpretations
- **Collision Area Bias**: How refs call wrestling, crusher tackles, ruck speed
- **Penalty Trends**: Teams drawing vs giving penalties at higher rates
- **Previous Controversies**: History between teams affecting intensity
- **Officiating Impact**: Potential for decisions to influence game flow

---

## C. CONTEXTUAL FACTORS

### C1. Timing & Scheduling
- **Game Timing**: Primetime (Friday/Saturday) vs Sunday games
- **Travel/Fatigue**: Long trips vs home games, bye week recovery
- **Fixture Congestion**: Games in back-to-back weeks vs staggered
- **Time Between Matches**: 5-day turnaround vs standard week
- **Seasonal Phase**: Early season vs finals contention vs post-Christmas

### C2. Venue & Environmental
- **Venue Characteristics**: Indoor vs outdoor, enclosed vs exposed conditions
- **Weather Forecasts**: Rain/wind/heat impact (suited teams vs not)
- **Altitude**: Non-factor in Australia but travel effects remain
- **Crowd Factors**: Home crowd support/intimidation factor
- **Ground Condition**: Wet grass affects possession, speed teams

### C3. External Factors
- **Media Narratives**: Dominant storylines affecting team focus
- **COVID/Health Protocols**: Ongoing health impacts on team availability
- **Contract Disputes**: Player negotiations affecting focus/morale
- **Franchise Movements**: Relocation impacts on team stability
- **Off-Field Incidents**: Judiciary cases, suspensions coming

---

## D. PLAYER-LEVEL FACTORS

### D1. Individual Form
- **Star Player Consistency**: Recent performance of team's best player(s)
- **Form Trajectory**: Players improving vs declining through season
- **Performance vs Specific Opponents**: Some players excel against certain teams
- **Match-Ups**: Star forwards vs opposing pack, halfback vs opposing defense

### D2. Key Position Impact
- **Fullback**: Field position authority, kick return ability
- **Halfback**: Playmaking, attack orchestration, goal kicking
- **Five-Eighth**: Backline creation, distribution quality
- **Prop/Hooker**: Ruck dominance, dummy half involvement
- **Lock**: Leadership, forward pack direction

### D3. Absence Impact
- **Replacement Quality**: Does backup adequately replicate the role?
- **Experience Gap**: Rookie replacements vs journeymen
- **Positional Criticality**: Some positions more impactful when injured
- **Cumulative Losses**: Multiple absences compounding

### D4. Emerging Form
- **Breakout Players**: Young talent making impact above expectations
- **Late-Season Bloomers**: Players hitting peak form in crucial moments
- **Return from Injury**: Sharpness after layoff
- **Age Advantage**: Veterans with experience vs hungry youth

---

## E. DECISION-MAKING FRAMEWORK

### E1. Weighting Strategy
Not all factors carry equal weight:
- **High Impact** (40-50% of decision): Recent form, H2H record, key injuries
- **Medium Impact** (30-40% of decision): Coaching quality, team cohesion, positional matchups
- **Contextual Impact** (10-20% of decision): Scheduling, venue, psychological factors

### E2. Confidence Levels
Assign confidence tiers to each prediction:
- **High Confidence**: Clear form advantage, multiple factors align
- **Medium Confidence**: Mixed signals, some uncertainty
- **Low Confidence**: Unpredictable, edge factors are minor

### E3. Value Considerations
In competition context:
- **Certainties**: Heavy favorites where most tippers agree (lower value)
- **Value Picks**: Underdogs with reasonable case (higher payout potential)
- **Contrarian Picks**: Low consensus picks (high reward if correct, risky)

### E4. Discipline
- **Avoid Emotional Picks**: Don't pick based on loyalty/hatred
- **Bias Recognition**: Know your blind spots (specific team preferences)
- **Data Over Gut**: Verify intuition against actual statistics
- **Change of Mind**: Be willing to revise if new information emerges (injuries, etc.)

---

## F. APPLICATION WORKFLOW

### Weekly Decision Process
1. **Compile Data**: Gather form, injury, H2H statistics for each matchup
2. **Analyze Matchups**: Compare positional strengths systematically
3. **Check Context**: Verify scheduling, venue, external factors
4. **Assess Psychology**: Team momentum, confidence levels
5. **Evaluate Value**: Consider odds vs confidence level
6. **Make Picks**: Document reasoning (helps improve over time)
7. **Review Outcomes**: Analyze why picks succeeded/failed for learning

### Tracking & Improvement
- Record picks and reasoning each week
- Track accuracy by factor (which considerations predicted best)
- Identify personal biases (teams you consistently mis-rate)
- Refine weighting based on historical accuracy
- Benchmark against consensus to evaluate contrarian success rate

## Summary

**Key Insight**: NRL tipping is not purely statistical—it requires balancing:
- **Data** (form, injuries, matchups)
- **Psychology** (team cohesion, confidence, desperation)
- **Context** (scheduling, venue, narratives)
- **Human Judgment** (recognizing when stats don't capture intangibles)

The best tippers systematically evaluate all three areas while remaining disciplined about biases.
